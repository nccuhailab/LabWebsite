Research

We conduct basic and applied research to investigate the design of embodied AI systems that assist and collaborate with people. Our research focuses on enhancing collaboration and synergy between humans and advanced technologies through three key domains.

Our Research Focus

At the Human Automation Interaction Lab, we explore the intersection of human cognition, artificial intelligence, and robotic systems. Our interdisciplinary approach combines insights from human factors, cognitive science, computer science, and engineering to create technologies that truly understand and augment human capabilities.

Our research is organized around three interconnected themes that collectively advance our understanding of human-machine collaboration. Each research area contributes unique perspectives and methodologies, while sharing the common goal of developing systems that are intuitive, adaptive, and aligned with human needs.

Research Areas

Human Robot Interaction

Exploring adaptive and interdependent actions between humans and humanoid robots, working cohesively toward shared objectives. We investigate coordination strategies, anticipatory control, and multimodal behavior modeling.

Learn more

Electroencephalography

Using EEG to capture real-time brain activity and understand cognitive processes in human-automation interaction scenarios. We focus on cognitive load assessment, trust calibration, and attention dynamics.

Learn more

Human Automation Interaction

Creating intuitive and efficient collaborations between humans and automated systems through adaptability, trust, and cognitive alignment. Our research spans cultural factors, multi-agent coordination, and dynamic task switching.

Learn more

Collaborative Opportunities

We welcome collaborations with researchers, industry partners, and students interested in advancing the field of human-automation interaction. Our lab provides a supportive environment for exploring innovative ideas and conducting rigorous research that makes a real-world impact.
